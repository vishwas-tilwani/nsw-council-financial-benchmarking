### 🏛️ [NSW Local Government Financial Benchmarking](https://github.com/vishwas-tilwani/nsw-council-financial-benchmarking)
Comparative P&L analysis of 6 Sydney councils (FY24 vs FY25) — benchmarking revenue,
expenditure and budget variance to identify financial performance patterns across local government.

---

## 🗂️ Project Overview

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Tool Used** | Microsoft Excel (Advanced) |
| **Councils Analysed** | City of Sydney · City of Parramatta · The Hills Shire · Hornsby · North Sydney · Woollahra |
| **Data Coverage** | FY2024 vs FY2025 Actuals + Budget |
| **Key Metrics** | Operating Result · Net Profit % · Underlying NP before Grants · YoY Movements · Budget Variance |
| **Deliverable** | Individual council dashboards + Comparative KPI benchmarking model |

---

## ❓ Business Questions

1. Which councils are the strongest financial performers by operating result and margin?
2. How did each council perform against their FY25 budget?
3. Which councils improved or deteriorated year-on-year?
4. How reliant are councils on capital grants vs self-funded operations?
5. Which councils show sustainability risk based on underlying profitability?
6. How do expense growth trends compare across the region?

## 🗺️ Dashboard Structure

This workbook is built as a **multi-page interactive model** with the following sections:

### 📍 Interactive Map Page
An overview map of Greater Sydney showing all 6 council locations with
**clickable navigation buttons** — click any council to jump directly
to its individual dashboard page.

### 📊 Individual Council Dashboards *(one per council)*
Each council has its own dedicated page including:
- Full income statement (FY24 Actual · FY25 Budget · FY25 Actual)
- Year-on-year movement analysis
- Budget variance (vs Budget)
- KPI summary panel
- Multi-series visual: Revenue · Expenses · Operating Result · Profit Margin over time

### 📈 Comparative KPI Dashboard
A single cross-council benchmarking view showing:
- Net Profit % over total revenue
- Underlying NP % before capital grants
- Direct operating result %
- Total revenues & operating results side-by-side
- Relative strengths, weaknesses & sustainability flags per council

## 💡 Council-by-Council Findings

### 🏙️ City of Sydney
- Largest revenue base at **$900M+**, consistently producing a strong surplus
- Very **low reliance on capital grants** — strength comes from user charges and interest income
- Margins held steady at **14–17%** despite rising expenses
- ✅ **Verdict: Most stable and self-sustaining council — benchmark model**

### 🏙️ City of Parramatta
- Revenue **declined YoY** from $472M to $439M
- FY25 beat budget, but driven by interest income — not operational growth
- Operating performance **weakened due to rising expenses**
- Underlying NP before grants turned **negative** — sustainability pressure flagged
- ⚠️ **Verdict: Needs cost-trend review and targeted revenue strategy**

### 🏘️ The Hills Shire Council
- **Highest operating result at $196M**, with strongest margins of **47–53%**
- Key driver: very large capital grant receipts
- Even after removing grants, Hills remains **strongly profitable**
- ✅ **Verdict: Standout performer — financially resilient**

### 🌿 Hornsby Shire Council
- **Biggest YoY turnaround** — moved from **-$28M deficit** (FY24) to **+$62.5M surplus** (FY25)
- Driven by grant recovery from prior-year negative adjustment
- Underlying NP before grants improved to **~$20.6M** — genuine operational recovery
- 🔄 **Verdict: Strong recovery but grant-driven — needs monitoring**

### 🌉 North Sydney Council
- Operating result improved slightly but underlying profit before grants was just **$1.3M**
- Operations are **almost break-even** — very thin margin
- User charges **declined YoY** — weakening service-based revenue
- ⚠️ **Verdict: Fragile — sensitive to revenue shocks or cost increases**

### 🏡 Woollahra Municipal Council
- Revenue grew modestly, driven by investment income and capital grants
- Operating results **declined 28% YoY** due to rising expenses
- Underlying NP before grants at just **1% of revenue**
- ⚠️ **Verdict: Thin margins and sustainability risk if trends persist**

## 🎯 Key Recommendations

| Council | Recommendation |
|---------|---------------|
| **Hills** | Maintain current strategy — focus on capital-grant efficiency |
| **Sydney** | Use as benchmark model for revenue diversification |
| **Hornsby** | Strengthen underlying operations to sustain recovery without grants |
| **North Sydney** | Grow user-charge revenue and tighten cost management |
| **Parramatta** | Cost-trend review + targeted revenue strategies |
| **Woollahra** | Pricing reviews, demand analysis and service efficiency reforms |

> 🌏 **Holistic Recommendation:** Implement a standardised cost-pressure dashboard
> across all councils for early risk signalling — particularly for councils showing
> thin or negative underlying results.
